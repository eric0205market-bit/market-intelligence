Morningstar's original moat framework was built empirically, not theoretically: screening data back to the 1960s for every company that sustained over 15% return on capital for more than 15 years, then observing that most such companies' advantages traced to intangible assets (brands, patents, government approvals), high switching costs, network effects, or scale/cost advantages.
